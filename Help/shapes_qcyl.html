Shapes: quartic cylinder

See also

Quartic cylinders are convex solids that resemble cylinders with rounded edges. They are geometrically related to quarts.

Syntax

A quartic cylinder is specified by its origin, or center, and each of its axes:

  • QCylinder(center, radius, material)
  • QCylinder(cx, cy, cz, radius, material)
  • QCylinder(cx, cy, cz, rx, ry, rz, material)

QCyl is accepted as synonym of QCylinder.

Primitive quartic cylinders are always aligned to the axes. If you need another orientation, you must apply the Rotate primitive to the quart. You can also apply a Scale operation to the rotated quart. Note, however, it doesn't make sense to scale a non-rotated quart, since the same effect could be achieved by modifying the size of its axes.
