Once you have rendered a scene, you can extract more information from it using the rendered image and the internal scene tree, which is internally stored by the Image Window.

Zooming and panning

You can zoom in and out any image on this window using the mouse wheel, as in Windows' image viewer.

You can use the mouse for zooming and panning:

GestureAction
Mouse wheelZooms the image in and out.
Control + Mouse wheelZooms the image in and out.
Double clickRestores the image to its original size.

You can also use the keyboard to perform these operations:

ShortcutAction
NumPad+Zooms in.
NumPad-Zooms out.
NumPad*Restores original size.
Arrow keysPans the image, when it is greater than the current window.
Control + Arrow keysPans the image at a faster pace.
HomeShows the left upper corner of the image.
EndShows the right lower corner of the image.

The status bar

The Image Window keeps an internal copy of the last rendered scene. When you move the mouse over the bitmap, the scene is used for calculating which point in the 3D scene your mouse is pointing to. The result of this query is shown in the status bar:

The first three panels in the status bar show the x, y and z coordinates of the hit point. The fourth panel contains the row and column in the bitmap, counting from the lower leftmost corner, and the last panel shows the distance, in the scene space, from the camera location to the hit point.

Note
When you move the mouse pointer over the scene background, no distance is shown since, by definition, the distance from the camera to the background is infinite.

If you press the Control key while moving the cursor over the image, the last panel will show the normal vector at the hit point:

If you press both the Control and the Shift keys while moving the mouse, the last panel will show the cosine of the angle formed between the eye ray and the normal vector at the hit point:

Finally, you can hold the Shift key in order to show the RGB components, in hexadecimal notation, of the pixel below the cursor:

If the color of the selected pixel matches or almost matches any of the named colors from SILLY, the color name is also shown.

In addition, you can copy the hit point position as a SILLY vector by pressing Ctrl+Shift+C, or by executing the Copy position as text command from the popup menu.
